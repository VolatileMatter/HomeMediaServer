🎮 What Is This?

Word Mastermind is an open-source Wordle clone built before the NYT acquired Wordle and before dozens of clones flooded the market. It distinguishes itself with practical self-hosting features: custom dictionary support, configurable word length (not just 5 letters), and multiple language support via swappable JSON word lists.

The core gameplay is identical to Wordle — guess a hidden word in 6 attempts, receiving colour-coded feedback after each guess. Green means the right letter in the right position; yellow means the letter is in the word but in the wrong position; grey means the letter is not in the word. The game is endless — no daily reset, no limit on plays per session.

Having a self-hosted version means no NYT paywall, no account required, and the ability to add custom word lists for themed games — technical jargon, fantasy vocabulary, or any domain-specific vocabulary set in JSON format.

📖 How to Play
  • Type a 5-letter word and press Enter to submit your guess
  • 🟩 Green: correct letter, correct position
  • 🟨 Yellow: correct letter, wrong position
  • ⬛ Grey: letter not in the word
  • Use the feedback to narrow down possibilities — 6 guesses total
  • Press New Game after winning or losing to play again immediately

🏆 Statistics

The game maintains a local statistics panel (identical to Wordle's) tracking: games played, win percentage, current streak, max streak, and a guess distribution histogram. These live in localStorage and are per-browser only.

No shared leaderboard exists. For competitive play among the friend group, compare streaks and average guess counts manually — the statistics panel makes this straightforward. A hub leaderboard integration would require hooking into the game's win callback, which is feasible given the simple JS codebase.
