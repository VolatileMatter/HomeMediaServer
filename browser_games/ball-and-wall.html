🎮 What Is This?

Ball And Wall is a Arkanoid/Breakout clone — a genre that traces back to Atari's 1976 Breakout and its 1986 Arkanoid refinement. The gameplay is deceptively simple: a ball bounces around the screen, and you control a paddle at the bottom to prevent it from falling off the edge. The ball destroys bricks on contact, and the goal is to clear every brick on the level.

What distinguishes a good Breakout clone from a mediocre one is the physics — specifically how the ball's angle changes based on where it strikes the paddle, and how brick destruction flows. Ball And Wall gets this right with smooth, consistent physics that reward deliberate paddle positioning over pure reaction time. Power-ups add variety to later levels.

Short sessions (5–15 minutes per attempt), naturally competitive high scores, and a skill ceiling that rewards practice all make it a good fit for a hub that sees casual drop-in play.

📖 How to Play
  • Move the paddle left and right to keep the ball in play
  • The ball destroys bricks on contact — clear all bricks to advance to the next level
  • Ball angle varies based on where it strikes the paddle — use this to aim
  • Catch power-ups that fall when certain bricks are destroyed
  • You have a limited number of lives — losing the ball costs one life
  • Game ends when all lives are lost
