🔐 Step 3 — Access Control

Served under the Browser Games hub. Access requires Tailscale enrollment and a valid Authentik SSO session. No additional Caddy block needed — inherits the hub's forward_auth.

💡
A Dark Room saves progress to the browser's localStorage. Saves are per-browser, not per-user at the server level. Different browsers / incognito sessions will start fresh.

🕯️ What Is This?

A Dark Room is a text-based incremental adventure by Michael Townsend (doublespeakgames), first released in 2013. It is one of the most quietly affecting browser games ever made — it begins as a bare idle game and slowly reveals itself as something stranger and more meaningful.

Play starts with three options and no explanation. As you interact, the world expands: a hut becomes a village, scavenging runs turn into armed expeditions, and a larger narrative begins to crystallise from the fragments you collect. The less you know going in, the better the experience.

The game has been praised for its minimalist aesthetic, its pacing, and the way its mechanics carry emotional weight. It was later ported to iOS and Android, but the browser original remains the canonical version.

⚠️
Avoid spoilers. The game's structure is part of the experience. Recommended to play cold.
⌨️ Controls

Entirely mouse-driven. Click buttons to take actions. The game is designed to be played in a browser tab — no keyboard shortcuts required. Works well on touchscreens too.
