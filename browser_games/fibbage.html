🎮 What Is This?

Fibbage is Jackbox's flagship bluffing party game — the format that launched the entire Jackbox Party Pack series. Each round a question appears with a blanked-out answer (e.g. "In 1987, the world record for largest ____ was set in Ohio"). Every player privately submits a fake-but-plausible answer. Those fakes are then shuffled with the real answer and everyone votes on which one they think is correct.

Points come from two places: fooling other players into voting for your fake (they score for you) and correctly identifying the true answer. The bluffing layer is what makes it special — a convincing liar who understands what sounds plausible can consistently beat players with better trivia knowledge. Writing a fake that's obviously wrong is easy; writing one that passes is an art.

This self-hosted clone replicates that loop without needing Jackbox TV, the PC game, or a streaming session. Players join from any browser on the Tailnet — phones work particularly well for submitting answers without anyone else seeing your screen.

📖 How to Play
  • One player creates a room and shares the join link — all players join before starting
  • Each round: a fill-in-the-blank trivia question appears
  • All players privately type a fake-but-convincing answer
  • All answers (including the real one) are shown — everyone votes for which is true
  • Points for: getting others to vote for your fake, and correctly identifying the real answer
  • Play a set number of rounds — highest score wins
⚠️ Known Limitations
IssueDetail
Question setThe bundled question database may be smaller than the official Jackbox game. Check the repo for the question count and any ability to add custom questions.
Minimum 3 playersFibbage requires at least 3 players — with only 2 there's only one fake answer and one real, making voting trivial.
No spectator modeDepending on implementation, players who join late may not be able to join mid-round.
👥 All players use their own device as a controller — phones are ideal. One screen can be shared/mirrored as a "TV" display for the main game view.
🎮 Party Setup Tips
SetupDetail
In-personMirror the host's screen on a TV. Everyone uses their phone to join and submit answers — the shared screen shows the voting results dramatically.
RemoteHost shares their screen via Discord/Zoom; everyone else joins the game on their own device. Both the shared screen and each player's device update in real time.
Optimal count4–6 players — enough fake answers to make voting genuinely difficult, not so many that the round drags.
