🎮 What Is This?

Particle Clicker was created at CERN's 2014 Webfest hackathon as a Cookie Clicker–style idle game with actual particle physics content. The gameplay loop mirrors Cookie Clicker precisely — click the collider to generate data, spend data on researchers and upgrades, automate production, and unlock new physics discoveries.

What sets it apart from a pure idle game is the educational layer. The upgrade tree follows real particle physics history: muons, pions, kaons, the W and Z bosons, and eventually the Higgs. Each discovery comes with a brief explanation of what it represents. It's a genuinely clever way to make the scale and timeline of high-energy physics research tangible — the progression from "one grad student clicking" to "hundreds of researchers running the LHC" mirrors how real physics collaborations work.

The idle mechanics mean it's well suited as a background tab game — leave it running during a session, check back when notifications appear. A full run to the Higgs takes several hours of real time.

📖 How to Play
  • Click the particle collider to generate data manually
  • Spend data to hire researchers — they generate data automatically over time
  • Purchase upgrades to multiply data production rates
  • Reach data milestones to trigger particle discoveries
  • Each discovery unlocks new researchers and upgrades, accelerating the loop
  • The game can run offline — close the tab and data accumulates until you return
