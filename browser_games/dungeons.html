  dungeons:

🎮 What Is This?

Realm of Dungeons is an idle multiplayer RPG — the kind of game you check in on rather than actively play. You create a character, assign them to training regimens that improve their stats over real time, and send them on dungeon expeditions that play out server-side while you're away. Return later to collect loot, level up, and send them on harder dungeons.

The multiplayer element is primarily the shared leaderboard and the social comparison it enables — seeing where your character ranks against other players on the server, and competing for the top positions over weeks of incremental progress. It's the same appeal as games like Runescape's idle skilling or Progress Quest, but with a friend group providing the competitive framing.

The low time commitment is the key feature: check in for 5 minutes, queue some training and dungeon runs, log out. No obligation to grind sessions. Well suited to a server where people have varying availability.

⚠️ Known Limitations
IssueDetail
MongoDB backup criticalAll character progress lives in MongoDB. Back up the Docker volume regularly — a lost database means everyone starts over.
Separate account systemRealm of Dungeons manages its own accounts — Authentik gates network access only.
Server-side idleThe idle progression runs server-side, not client-side — the server must be running for characters to progress. Downtime pauses everyone's progress.
👥 Shared server with individual accounts — competition is via leaderboard rather than real-time interaction.

ComponentDetail
BackendNode.js API handling character progression, dungeon logic, and leaderboard
