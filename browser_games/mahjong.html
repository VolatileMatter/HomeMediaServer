🎮 What Is This?

Mahjong Solitaire — often simply called Mahjong in Western contexts — is a single-player tile-matching patience game with no relation to the competitive four-player Mahjong played in East Asia beyond sharing the same tile set. The game presents a stack of tiles arranged in a preset pattern. Tiles are removed in matching pairs, but only "free" tiles can be selected — a tile is free if it has no tile covering it and if at least one of its left or right sides is not blocked by another tile.

The puzzle-solving aspect comes from choosing which pairs to remove in which order. Removing the wrong pair can block others and make the board unsolvable. Planning several moves ahead is rewarded; careless matching can paint you into a corner. A shuffle or restart option exists for when the board locks up.

Green Mahjong has a clean, restful aesthetic — muted green tones, clear tile graphics, and no time pressure by default. It's one of the most genuinely relaxing games in the hub, suitable for a slow afternoon session.

📖 How to Play
  • Click a free tile to select it (highlights)
  • Click a matching free tile to remove both — free means not covered and at least one open side
  • Continue until all tiles are removed (win) or no valid pairs remain (stuck)
  • If stuck, use Shuffle to rearrange remaining tiles, or New Game to restart
  • Multiple layout presets are available — the traditional Turtle layout is a good starting point

🏆 Time Tracking

Green Mahjong tracks your best completion time per layout in localStorage. Comparing best times on the same layout among the friend group is the natural informal competition — "I cleared the Turtle in 4:32, can you beat it?" Time is the cleanest metric for this type of puzzle since the board layout is fixed.
