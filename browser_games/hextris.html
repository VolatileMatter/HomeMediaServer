🎮 What Is This?

Hextris was created in 2014 as an open-source project and immediately attracted attention for its clean geometric aesthetic and surprisingly deep mechanics. Where Tetris uses a falling block metaphor on a flat grid, Hextris wraps the playfield around a hexagon — blocks fall inward from all six sides simultaneously, and the hexagon rotates under player control.

The colour-matching mechanic adds a layer not present in Tetris: it's not just about placement but about sequencing. Three adjacent same-coloured blocks clear in a chain reaction, and chaining clears together multiplies the score. High-level play involves planning several rotations ahead to set up cascades.

Games are typically short — 2 to 5 minutes before the difficulty overwhelms — which makes it well-suited to quick play sessions between friends and natural high-score competition.

📖 How to Play
  • Rotate the hexagon left or right to position the face where the next block should land
  • Blocks fall inward from outside — they stack on the hexagon face they hit
  • Match 3 or more same-coloured blocks in a row to clear them
  • Chain clears for score multipliers — setting up cascades is key
  • The game ends when any stack reaches the centre of the hexagon
  • Speed increases over time — the longer you survive, the faster it gets

🏆 Leaderboard

The original Hextris codebase saves a single best score per browser via localStorage. Scores are not submitted anywhere and are local to each player's browser.

Hextris is a strong candidate for a shared leaderboard integration — it's a natural high-score game and the short session length encourages repeat play. A score hook could be added by patching the gameOver() function in the source to POST to the hub's score API. Document that work here if undertaken.
