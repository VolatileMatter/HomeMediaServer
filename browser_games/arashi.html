🎮 What Is This?

Arashi is a faithful JavaScript clone of Tempest — one of Atari's most innovative arcade games from 1981. The original was notable for being among the first games to use a colour vector display and one of the first to offer a choice of starting level. Tempest introduced a distinctive perspective: rather than a flat playfield, the game takes place on the surface of various geometric shapes — tubes, webs, flat planes — viewed from one end.

The player controls a claw-shaped shooter that travels around the rim of the shape. Enemies — Flippers, Tankers, Spikers — crawl up from the far end toward the rim. The shooter fires down the tube to destroy them before they reach the top. Surviving longer waves reveals increasingly complex tube geometries and faster, more varied enemy patterns.

Arashi is described as a "high-quality" clone — it captures the feel of the original well, with smooth movement, correct enemy behaviour, and the escalating pressure of the later waves. The arcade score-attack format makes it a natural high-score competition game.

📖 How to Play
  • Move the shooter around the rim of the tube using the arrow keys
  • Fire down the tube to destroy enemies before they reach the rim
  • Enemies at the rim will grab and kill your shooter — don't let them reach it
  • Clear all enemies to advance to the next wave with a new tube shape
  • Spikes left by Spikers on the tube surface must be avoided when warping to the next level
  • Superzapper destroys all enemies on screen — use it sparingly
Controls
← → Move shooter around the tube rim Space / Z Fire down the tube X Superzapper (screen clear, limited uses)
