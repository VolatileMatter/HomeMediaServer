🎮 What Is This?

This is a faithful browser port of Asteroids — the 1979 Atari arcade game that became one of the highest-grossing arcade titles of all time and helped define the twin-stick shooter genre. The implementation by Doug McInnes uses HTML5 Canvas and vanilla JavaScript to recreate the original's vector-graphics aesthetic: white wireframe shapes on a black background, with the signature physics of Newtonian momentum in zero gravity.

The gameplay is deceptively simple and deeply skill-expressive. Your ship has momentum — turning and thrusting changes velocity, it doesn't teleport you. Bullets travel in the direction you were facing when you fired. Large asteroids split into medium ones, which split into small ones; you're never safe until the screen is clear. The wraparound screen edges mean threats can come from any direction.

As a short-session score-attack game it's ideal for casual high-score competition in a friend group. Each run takes 2–10 minutes depending on skill, making it easy to pass around.

📖 How to Play
  • Rotate your ship to face the direction you want to travel, then thrust to accelerate
  • Shoot asteroids to break them — large → medium → small → destroyed
  • Small asteroids are worth the most points but are hardest to hit
  • The screen wraps — objects exiting one edge appear at the opposite side
  • A UFO occasionally crosses the screen and fires at you — prioritise it
  • You have 3 lives; the game ends when all are lost
Controls
← → Rotate ship left / right ↑ Thrust (add velocity in facing direction) Space Fire Z Hyperspace (random teleport — risky)
