🎮 What Is This?

Zero-K is one of the finest free RTS games ever made — built on the Spring engine (itself a free-software reimplementation of Total Annihilation's engine) and developed by a dedicated community over more than a decade. It features over 100 unique units, full terrain deformation, realistic physics simulation for projectiles and wrecks, and a tactical depth that rivals commercial RTS games costing £40.

What sets Zero-K apart mechanically is its physics-first approach. Units have realistic weights and can push each other. Explosions throw units around. Terrain elevation meaningfully affects combat. Artillery has genuine ballistic arcs affected by terrain. Most RTS games abstract these interactions — Zero-K simulates them, which produces emergent moments that no designer explicitly programmed.

The learning curve is honest: Zero-K is deep and takes time to understand. The campaign and AI skirmish modes provide a good on-ramp. Once a friend group all reaches intermediate competence, 2v2 or 3v3 team games become genuinely spectacular.
