🎮 What Is This?

A Dark Room is a browser-based text adventure and incremental resource management game created by Michael Townsend (Doublespeak Games). It began as an open-source project in 2013 and gained a significant following for its atmospheric minimalism and the way it withholds information — not as a puzzle, but as an aesthetic choice.

The game opens with almost nothing. A single button. A dark room. What it becomes over the next hour of play is the game's central secret. The writing is sparse and evocative; the mechanics layer in gradually rather than front-loading tutorials. Players who bounce off it in the first five minutes often find themselves completely absorbed by the time new systems unlock.

It was later ported to iOS and Android, where it became a quiet cult hit. The browser version on GitHub remains the original and requires no modifications to run.

📖 How to Play
  • Click the single button on screen to begin — let things unfold naturally
  • Resources accumulate over time; spend them to unlock buildings and items
  • New panels appear as the game progresses — explore each one when it opens
  • The game autosaves continuously to localStorage
  • A full run takes roughly 1–3 hours depending on play style
  • There is a New Game option in the menu — use it only if you want to restart from scratch
