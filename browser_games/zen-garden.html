🎮 What Is This?

Zen Garden simulates the act of raking a Japanese rock garden (karesansui). Your cursor becomes a rake, and dragging it through the sand canvas leaves parallel grooves behind. The lines curve gracefully to flow around any stones you've placed, mimicking the way real zen garden raking flows around obstacles.

The traditional Japanese rock garden is a meditative art form — the act of raking is itself the practice, not the end result. This digital version captures that spirit: slow, deliberate movements produce satisfying patterns; hurried scribbling produces chaos. It rewards patience.

From the science-art GitHub project, which focuses on browser-based scientific and artistic simulations. The aesthetic is appropriately minimal — beige sand, dark grooves, round stones.

📖 How to Use
  • Click and drag slowly to rake lines through the sand
  • Lines curve automatically around placed stones — don't fight them
  • Right-click (or use a tool selector) to place or remove stones
  • Move slowly for clean, smooth lines; fast movement produces irregular grooves
  • Screenshot to preserve a pattern you like before refreshing
Controls
Click + Drag Rake lines through the sand Right Click Place or remove a stone (check build) C / Reset Clear the sand back to a flat state
