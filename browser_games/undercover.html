Undercover

// Social Deduction · Word Game · Multiplayer · Static Files (WebRTC) · Tailscale + Authentik forward_auth //

A browser implementation of the social deduction word game. Each player receives a secret word — most get the same word, but one or two "undercover" players receive a similar but different word. Players describe their word without revealing it; the group votes on who they think is undercover. Runs peer-to-peer via WebRTC — no game server required.

⚠️ Architecture Note — WebRTC (No Game Server)
💡
Undercover uses WebRTC peer-to-peer for all multiplayer communication — there is no centralised game server. The static files are served by the hub, but game state is exchanged directly between browsers. This means the hub's forward_auth is the only gate needed. However, WebRTC requires a STUN/TURN server for peer discovery — over Tailscale (where all peers are on the same virtual network), this should work without external STUN.

🎮 What Is This?

Undercover is a word-based social deduction game. Each player receives a secret word — the majority share the same word, but one or two "undercover" players receive a closely related but different word. For example, the majority might have "Coffee" while undercover players have "Tea". Players take turns saying a single word or phrase that describes their secret word without revealing it directly.

The tension comes from the undercover players trying to blend in — they must give clues that sound plausible for the majority word without knowing exactly what it is, using only the descriptions they've heard from others. After everyone has spoken, players vote on who they think is undercover. The undercover player wins by surviving until only two players remain; the majority wins by voting out all undercover players.

It produces the same kind of social pressure and bluffing as Secret Hitler but in a much shorter, lighter format — a typical round takes 10–15 minutes. Excellent for warm-up before longer games.

📖 How to Play
  • One player creates a room and shares the join link with 3–9 others
  • Each player is privately shown their secret word on their own device
  • Taking turns, each player says one word or phrase describing their secret word
  • After all players have spoken, discuss and vote on who is undercover
  • The voted-out player reveals their word — majority wins if they were undercover, game continues otherwise
  • Undercover wins by surviving until 2 players remain

🔧 Troubleshooting Peer Connections

If players can't connect to each other:

  1. Confirm all players are on the Tailscale network
  2. Open browser DevTools → Console and look for ICE connection errors
  3. Try refreshing all clients and having the host recreate the room
  4. If Tailscale is not available, the game may need an external STUN server configured in its source
