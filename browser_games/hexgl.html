🎮 What Is This?

HexGL is a futuristic anti-gravity racing game created by Thibaut Despoulain in 2012 as a technical showcase for Three.js and WebGL. It was one of the first browser games to demonstrate that a visually complex, GPU-accelerated 3D racing game was achievable entirely in the browser without plugins — a significant landmark at the time.

The game is directly inspired by the WipEout and F-Zero series: hoverships, neon-lit circuits, high-speed gameplay, and the characteristic challenge of managing momentum while navigating tight turns at full speed. Unlike driving games, hoverships don't have conventional friction — momentum carries you through corners, and oversteering is a constant risk.

The experience holds up exceptionally well over a decade later. The three-minute lap time per track makes it suitable for quick sessions, and lap time competition is a natural informal leaderboard for a friend group.

📖 How to Play
  • Select a track and difficulty, then launch
  • The ship accelerates automatically — focus on steering
  • Stay on the track — going off-track reduces speed dramatically
  • Collect speed boosts on the track surface for temporary acceleration
  • Complete all laps with the best time possible
  • Higher difficulty means faster base speed and tighter handling
Controls
← → Steer left / right ↑ Brake / decelerate Space Airbrake (sharper turn assist) A / D Alternate steer keys
⚠️ Known Limitations
IssueDetail
WebGL requiredAll modern browsers support WebGL, but very old hardware or integrated graphics may produce low framerates. A dedicated GPU provides the best experience.
Keyboard requiredCompetitive play requires keyboard. Mobile touch controls exist but precision is insufficient for tight lines.
Best times are browser-localLap times stored in localStorage — not shared across users.
Archived repoLast updated ~2013. Stable and visually complete. Three.js API changes may cause minor issues on very new browsers — check console if rendering breaks.

🏆 Lap Time Competition

Lap times are the natural competition metric — sharing and comparing times on a specific track and difficulty setting is straightforward. "My best on the main track at medium is 2:47 — can you beat it?" is a clean informal leaderboard.

A hub leaderboard integration would be excellent here — HexGL fires a clear game-over/lap-complete event that could POST the lap time to the hub's score API. Document that work here if undertaken.
