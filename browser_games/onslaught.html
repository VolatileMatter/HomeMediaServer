🎮 What Is This?

Onslaught! Arena is a top-down arena shooter created by Lost Decade Games in 2011 and released as open source. At the time of release it was held up as a landmark example of what HTML5 Canvas could do — a polished, performant, full-featured game with sprite animation, audio, multiple weapons, and escalating enemy AI, all running natively in the browser without Flash.

The game drops you into an enclosed arena with a sword and sets monsters loose. Weapon pickups appear as you kill enemies — bows for range, wands for area effect, axes for power. Waves escalate in enemy count and variety; surviving past wave 10 requires actively managing pickups and movement patterns.

It's a short-session arcade game: most runs last 5–15 minutes. The wave number and score create natural competition among friends playing on the same server.

📖 How to Play
  • Move through the arena and attack nearby enemies with your current weapon
  • Weapon pickups appear on the ground — walk over them to equip
  • Each wave ends when all enemies are dead; the next wave starts automatically
  • Later waves introduce faster, tougher, and more numerous enemies
  • Your score accumulates based on kills and waves survived
Controls
WASD / Arrow keys Move character Spacebar Attack (auto-targets nearest enemy) Walk over pickup Collect and equip weapon

🏆 Leaderboard

Onslaught! Arena tracks a local high score (wave reached + kill count) in localStorage. There's no cross-user leaderboard built in.

This is a strong candidate for hub leaderboard integration — the wave number and score are clean, comparable metrics. The game's game.js exposes a score variable that could be posted to the hub's score API on game over. Document that work here if undertaken.
