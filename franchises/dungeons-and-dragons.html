🛒 D&D Merchandise & Legacy
🎲 Early Years (1974–1983)

TSR’s original boxed sets, Chainmail miniatures rules, and early modules (Keep on the Borderlands, Tomb of Horrors) sold via hobby shops. First D&D action figures by LJN (1982–1985) — Warduke, Strongheart, and the animated series toys.

📚 The Novel Explosion (1988–2000)

Dragonlance and Forgotten Realms novels became bestsellers, with dozens of authors. TSR licensed board games (D&D Adventure System series), and later Hasbro expanded into video games via SSI, Interplay, and BioWare.

🃏 Modern Collectibles (2014–present)

WizKids miniatures, IDW comics, Nolzur’s Marvelous Miniatures, and D&D-branded apparel. The 2023 movie renewed mainstream merchandise, with Hasbro’s “D&D Beyond” digital tools becoming the central hub.

📖 D&D History — From Wargaming to Global Phenomenon
1974–1977: The Birth

Gary Gygax and Dave Arneson merge Chainmail miniatures rules with fantasy storytelling. The original white box sells out at hobby shops.

1983–1997: The Satantic Panic & TSR Expansion

AD&D 1E and 2E released; novels and cartoon reach mass market. TSR overextends and is purchased by Wizards of the Coast in 1997.

2000–2012: Hasbro Era & 3E/4E

3rd Edition redefines the game. D&D Online and Neverwinter Nights bring digital to the forefront. 4E splits the player base.

2014–Present: 5E Renaissance

5E becomes the most successful edition, fueled by Critical Role, Stranger Things, and Baldur’s Gate 3. D&D Beyond goes digital-first.
