Five Nights at Freddy's

// PC · Mobile · VR · Console · Books · Movie · Fan Games · 2014–Present //

Scott Cawthon's indie horror megafranchise — launched in 2014 as a $5 GameJolt game about surviving animatronics, grew into one of gaming's most intricate unsolved lore puzzles and a genuine multimedia empire. The core games span FNaF 1 through Security Breach + Ruin; the extended universe includes 20+ anthology novels, a theatrical film, fan games that became ascended canon, and a VR subseries. Use the Library tab to browse every entry by release order, story arc, or platform. 🛒 FNaF Merchandise & Collectibles
📦 2014–2015 — The Initial Wave

FNaF merchandise launched almost immediately after the game's success. The first official products were plush toys produced by Funko — the Freddy, Bonnie, Chica, and Foxy plush set sold out almost immediately and remained scarce for over a year. Hot Topic became the primary physical retail channel for FNaF apparel, selling out of FNaF shirts within days of restocking. The initial Funko Pop! figures (Freddy Fazbear, Bonnie, Chica, Foxy, Golden Freddy) launched in 2015 and became some of Funko's fastest-selling new licenses.

🧸 2016–2019 — The Funko Explosion & Action Figures

Funko expanded the FNaF Pop! line aggressively — eventually producing over 50 distinct FNaF Funko Pop! figures, covering every animatronic from every game including exclusives and chase variants. McFarlane Toys entered with construction sets and action figures. The Plushstrap 4" plush became a particularly sought-after item. Sanshee and other licensees produced enamel pins, keychains, and apparel. The market for FNaF merchandise at conventions (PAX, SDCC) was enormous.

🎬 2023 — Movie Merchandise Wave

The 2023 film triggered a second major merchandise wave. Film-accurate versions of Freddy, Chica, Bonnie, and Foxy appeared alongside standard game-based products. Spirit Halloween — which has an existing relationship with FNaF through annual licensed costumes — sold film-specific animatronic prop replicas. Funko released film-edition Pops featuring the human cast. The Spirit Halloween FNaF partnership is particularly notable: Spirit Halloween's seasonal stores are a direct real-world analogue to the franchise's horror-attraction themes, which FNaF 3 (Fazbear's Fright) literalized.

📖 Franchise History
📅 2012–2013 — Before the Bite
Chipper & Sons and the Accidental Horror Genre
Scott Cawthon was a Christian game developer making children's games. His 2013 title Chipper & Sons Lumber Co. received a devastating Steam review: critics noted the animal characters looked "terrifying" and "nightmarish" rather than charming. Rather than quit, Cawthon leaned in. He spent the next several months prototyping a game intentionally designed around animatronic horror — the design philosophy that would become FNaF. The original FNaF prototype showed security guard mechanics, limited power, and camera management in a rough form that closely resembled the shipped product.
📅 2014 — The Explosion
GameJolt to Cultural Phenomenon in 90 Days
Five Nights at Freddy's launched on GameJolt on August 8, 2014. It cost $5. Within a week, it was the most-covered game on YouTube — PewDiePie, Markiplier, and Jacksepticeye's Let's Play videos collectively drove tens of millions of views in the first month. The game launched on Steam within 48 hours and reached the top of the charts. FNaF 2 launched just three months later on November 10, 2014 — a development pace Cawthon maintained through the entire original trilogy and beyond. By December 2014, FNaF had generated more YouTube reaction content than any other game in the platform's history. The franchise became synonymous with Let's Play culture.
📅 2015 — The Golden Age
FNaF 3, FNaF 4, and the Birth of Lore Culture
2015 saw both FNaF 3 (March) and FNaF 4 (July) — two distinct tonal approaches. FNaF 3 introduced the Springtrap reveal and the first major lore confirmation about William Afton and the Missing Children. FNaF 4 added the Crying Child and the Bite of '87 subplot, dramatically deepening the timeline mystery. The FNaF Wiki and community theorist channels (MatPat's Game Theory in particular) brought the lore to audiences who had never played the games. MatPat's "FNAF is Real" theory video became one of the most-watched YouTube essays of the year. The fan game ecosystem exploded: One Night at Flumpty's, Five Nights at Candy's, and dozens of others launched in 2015. This ecosystem would become one of the defining characteristics of the franchise.
📅 2016–2018 — The Narrative Turn
Sister Location, the Afton Arc, and the End of the Beginning
Sister Location (2016) was the first major tonal pivot — scripted, voiced, narrative-forward. The Afton family became the emotional core of the series. Michael Afton, Baby/Elizabeth, and William's history added dramatic weight the gameplay-first entries hadn't attempted. Pizzeria Simulator (2017) was released free on Steam under the guise of a tycoon game — a deliberate misdirect. Its ending, where Henry Emily burns everything down and eulogizes the children, remains one of the most emotionally powerful moments in indie gaming. Ultimate Custom Night (2018) closed the original arc with William Afton in a purgatory of his own creation. The Cassette Man final cutscene felt definitive. Scott Cawthon appeared to be wrapping up the franchise — until VR changed that.
📅 2019–2021 — Steel Wool and New Directions
VR, the Mimic Seed, and the AR That Died
Help Wanted (2019) marked the handoff to Steel Wool Studios — a larger developer working under Cawthon's creative direction. The VR anthology was warmly received, and the Glitchtrap storyline introduced a new antagonist: William Afton digitized and escaped into the game's code, possessing a beta tester named Vanessa. FNaF AR: Special Delivery (2019) by Illumix attempted a mobile AR game — animatronics appearing in your real environment. It had a devoted player base but never found mainstream traction, and servers were shut down on January 13, 2023. Scott Cawthon publicly retired from active game development in June 2021 following personal controversy, handing the franchise fully to Steel Wool with his continued involvement as executive producer.
📅 2021–2023 — The Blumhouse Era
Security Breach, the Movie, and the Mimic Revealed
Security Breach launched December 2021 to mixed reception — technically ambitious, visually impressive, narratively messy with numerous unresolved threads. The "many endings" approach frustrated longtime lore followers. The Ruin DLC (2023) was widely seen as a course-correction. Free, focused, and culminating in the Mimic reveal — a digital entity that mimics Afton but is distinct from him — it retroactively clarified much of the Glitchtrap/Vanessa arc from Help Wanted. The FNaF theatrical film (October 2023) was the franchise's commercial peak: $297M worldwide on a $20M budget, one of the most profitable horror films of the decade. Josh Hutcherson and Matthew Lillard were universally praised. A sequel is in active development. Into the Pit (2024) by Mega Cat Studios was the first third-party-developed FNaF game not from Steel Wool — a critically acclaimed 2D pixel adventure adapting the Fazbear Frights story, showing the franchise had matured into a genuine licensed game platform.
📅 2024–Present
Where Things Stand
As of early 2026, the FNaF franchise is in one of its most active phases: a film sequel in development, Steel Wool continuing VR content (Help Wanted 2 released 2023), Into the Pit showing a new model for licensed third-party entries, and the Fazbear Frights / Tales from the Pizzaplex book series ongoing. The community lore debate continues — the Mimic / Glitchtrap / Afton relationship is still contested. MatPat's retirement from Game Theory in 2024 was marked by his final FNaF theory video, a cultural moment that trended worldwide. Scott Cawthon remains an executive producer and occasional public voice for the franchise. The FNaF Wiki remains one of the most active game wikis on Fandom.

🎲 Notable Facts & Trivia
  • FNaF 1 was created in approximately 6 weeks. Scott Cawthon began development after the Chipper & Sons review criticism and shipped before any major publisher knew the game existed.
  • At peak popularity (2015), FNaF merchandise at Hot Topic outsold every other licensed property in their stores — including major Marvel and DC properties.
  • The franchise generated an estimated $500M+ in merchandise revenue by 2020, dwarfing the games' actual revenue.
  • MatPat (Game Theory) has covered FNaF more than any other single topic in his channel's history — over 30 episodes across 10 years. His final FNaF video (2024) trended worldwide.
  • Scott Cawthon is a devout Christian and has stated multiple times that themes of redemption, sin, and consequence are intentionally embedded in the FNaF lore — particularly in Pizzeria Simulator's ending.
  • The Fazbear Frights anthology series has sold over 3 million copies collectively, making it one of the best-selling horror middle-grade book series in print.
  • The 2023 film used practical animatronic suits built by the Jim Henson Company — the same studio behind the Muppets. The film's behind-the-scenes confirmed all four main animatronics were fully physical.
  • FNaF AR: Special Delivery accumulated over 10 million downloads before its shutdown. It held a 4.5+ App Store rating at shutdown.
  • The Joy of Creation: Story Mode is the only FNaF fan game Scott Cawthon has publicly praised by name on record. He called it "far better than anything I could have made."
  • There are an estimated 3,000+ FNaF fan games catalogued on the FNaF Fan Game Wiki — making it one of the largest fan game ecosystems around any single IP in history.
