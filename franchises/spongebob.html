🛒 SpongeBob Merchandise
🍔 Plush & Toys (1999–present)

Thousands of plushies, McDonald's Happy Meal toys (over 10 different promotions), LEGO sets (Bikini Bottom, Krusty Krab, The Flying Dutchman), Funko Pops, and Jakks Pacific figures. The rarest are SDCC exclusives and first-wave plush.

📚 Books & Comics

Over 200+ Scholastic storybooks, chapter books, and comic books. SpongeBob Comics (United Plankton Pictures / Bongo Comics) ran 80+ issues (2011–2018) — directly overseen by Hillenburg and the only print series that counts as semi-official. Audiobook adaptations read by Tom Kenny. See the Library → Books & Comics view for the complete list.

🎭 Broadway Musical

The SpongeBob Musical — out-of-town tryout at Chicago's Oriental Theatre (June 2016); Broadway premiere at the Palace Theatre (November 2017). Songs commissioned from Sara Bareilles, Steven Tyler, Yolanda Adams, They Might Be Giants, Plain White T's, and others. Nominated for 12 Tony Awards (2018) including Best Musical. Original cast recording released by Sony Masterworks (2018) — available on Spotify, Apple Music, and Amazon Music. North American tour ran 2018–2019.

🎮 Board Games & TCG

Monopoly: SpongeBob SquarePants Edition, Operation: SpongeBob, Uno, and a short-lived TCG "SpongeBob SquarePants Trading Card Game" by Rittenhouse Archives (2003).

📖 History of SpongeBob
1984–1998 — Creation by Stephen Hillenburg

Marine biology teacher turned animator. Hillenburg developed SpongeBob for Nickelodeon after working on Rocko's Modern Life. The pilot "Bubblestand" / "Ripped Pants" aired May 1, 1999.

1999–2004 — Golden Age

Seasons 1–3 defined modern animation. The first movie (2004) was intended as a series finale but the show continued due to massive ratings.

2005–2015 — Post-Hillenburg & Revival

After Hillenburg stepped back, the show had a creative lull but regained momentum. Stephen Hillenburg returned in 2015 and remained executive producer until his death in 2018. The second movie (2015) revived theatrical interest.

2019–Present — Streaming & Expansion

Sponge on the Run (2020) launched Paramount+. Spin-offs Kamp Koral and The Patrick Star Show premiered. The franchise remains one of Nickelodeon's most profitable IPs, generating over $13 billion in retail sales.
