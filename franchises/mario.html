📖 Franchise History
1981
The Arcade Origin — Jumpman
Shigeru Miyamoto designed Donkey Kong as a response to failed negotiations to license Popeye for an arcade game. The protagonist — originally named Jumpman — was a carpenter, not a plumber. His girlfriend Pauline was kidnapped by a gorilla. The game became one of Nintendo's first major arcade hits in North America. Mario (the name came later, reportedly after Nintendo of America's warehouse owner Mario Segale) became the default protagonist for whatever Nintendo needed next.
1985
Super Mario Bros. — The Game That Saved the Industry
Released as a launch title for the Famicom's North American debut as the Nintendo Entertainment System, Super Mario Bros. is credited with reviving the video game industry after the 1983 crash. Miyamoto and Takashi Tezuka designed the first level, 1-1, as a masterclass in implicit game design — everything the player needs to understand the rules is taught without a single word of text. The game sold over 40 million copies bundled with the NES.
1988–1990
SMB2, SMB3, and the Peak of the NES Era
Nintendo of America rejected the Japanese SMB2 (The Lost Levels) as too hard for Western audiences and instead reskinned Doki Doki Panic with Mario characters. The "fake" SMB2 introduced four-character gameplay and Shy Guys — both have persisted in the franchise ever since. Super Mario Bros. 3 (1988 in Japan, 1990 in North America) is widely considered the finest 2D platformer of the 8-bit era and remains one of the best games ever made.
1990–1995
The SNES Golden Age
Super Mario World launched with the SNES and introduced Yoshi — one of Nintendo's most enduring characters. Super Mario World 2: Yoshi's Island (1995) used the Super FX2 chip to achieve its distinctive crayon-drawn aesthetic and established the "Baby Mario" prequel continuity. The SNES era also saw Nintendo license the character to DiC for three cartoon series — the less said the better, but the "Do the Mario" dance from the Super Show's credits is an objectively correct cultural artifact.
1993
The Live-Action Film — A Warning to Future Generations
Hollywood's first major video game adaptation was a $48 million disaster. Directed by Rocky Morton and Annabel Jankel, the film reimagined the Mushroom Kingdom as a parallel-dimension cyberpunk city where dinosaurs evolved into humanoids and Bowser was Dennis Hopper in a business suit. Bob Hoskins (Mario) and John Leguizamo (Luigi) both admitted to heavy drinking on set. Hoskins called it the worst thing he ever did. It grossed $21 million against its budget. Nintendo has never licensed a live-action Mario adaptation since.
1996
Super Mario 64 — The 3D Revolution
Super Mario 64 defined how 3D platformers work. Miyamoto's team had to invent solutions to problems no one had solved before — camera systems, analogue movement, spatial audio, level design for three dimensions. The result was a game that still holds up. The run animation alone — the way Mario's weight shifts — remains a reference point for character animation. 64 established the template that every 3D Mario since has iterated on.
2007–2010
Super Mario Galaxy — A Second Peak
Super Mario Galaxy used the Wii's motion controls purposefully rather than as a gimmick, introduced fully orchestrated music to the franchise, and built its world design around the concept of gravitational spheres — tiny planets with their own gravity. Mahito Yokota and Koji Kondo's score remains the most musically ambitious in Nintendo history. Galaxy 2 (2010) is often ranked above the original — it is denser, more inventive, and introduces Yoshi into the 3D Mario context for the first time.
2017
Super Mario Odyssey — Reinvention Again
Odyssey introduced Cappy, a hat that can capture and control enemies, as the core mechanic — an entirely new movement vocabulary built on top of the most technically precise Mario movement ever coded. The game's real-world-inspired kingdoms (New Donk City, the Luncheon Kingdom) gave it a surreal quality distinct from any previous Mario game. It sold over 26 million copies on Switch. The post-credits sequence is still being discussed.
2023
Double Victory — The Movie and Wonder
2023 was the best year for Mario since at least the Galaxy era. The Illumination animated film grossed $1.36 billion worldwide — the highest-grossing video game adaptation ever made and the second-highest-grossing animated film of all time at that point. Four months later, Super Mario Bros. Wonder launched on Switch to universal acclaim, widely regarded as the most inventive 2D Mario since Yoshi's Island. After thirty years, the 1993 film's legacy was officially buried.
🗺️ Canon Map
What's canon? What's not?

Nintendo has never published an official Mario timeline. The franchise is intentionally loose — Miyamoto has said the games are each their own distinct fairy tale. The sub-series (Paper Mario, Mario RPG, Mario Kart, Mario Party) are generally considered separate continuities from the mainline platformers. The DiC cartoons and 1993 film are non-canon by any measure. The 2023 animated film is not directly tied to any game's events.

🎲 Notable Facts & Trivia
  • Super Mario Bros. is the best-selling video game franchise of all time with over 800 million units sold across all titles.
  • The original SMB was designed in parallel with the NES hardware — some mechanics (the coin counter, the timer) existed to mask the hardware's limitations.
  • Shigeru Miyamoto designed the Mario Bros. level layout on graph paper on his commute to work.
  • Super Mario Bros. 3 was believed by many to be a "movie" due to the curtain and stage lighting — the game opens on a theatrical stage and the entire world is framed as a performance.
  • The "Super Mario Bros. speedrunning" community is one of the oldest continuous speedrunning communities in gaming, dating back to the early internet era.
  • Koji Kondo's Super Mario Bros. theme (the "ground theme") is one of the most recognizable pieces of music ever composed — it has been performed by symphony orchestras worldwide.
  • Bob Hoskins, when asked about the 1993 film in a 2007 Guardian interview, said simply: "The worst thing I ever did."
  • The 2023 film's $1.36 billion worldwide gross made it the highest-grossing video game adaptation in cinema history, surpassing Warcraft, Detective Pikachu, and Sonic the Hedgehog 2.
