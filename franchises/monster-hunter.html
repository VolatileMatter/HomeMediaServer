Monster Hunter

// PS2 · PSP · 3DS · Switch · PC · Mobile · Jellyfin //

Capcom's action-RPG franchise about hunting massive creatures, harvesting their parts, and using those parts to craft better gear to hunt bigger creatures. Running since 2004, it spent its first decade as a Japanese cult hit before Monster Hunter: World (2018) broke it worldwide — now one of the top-10 best-selling game franchises ever. Absolute newcomer? Start with Monster Hunter: World on PC (Steam). It's €30, runs on most rigs, has 500+ hours of content, and is the series' most accessible entry point by a wide margin. Only go backward into PSP/3DS games after you know you love the loop.
📺 TV & Anime
Monster Hunter Stories (Anime)

Studio: David Production  |  Year: 2016–2018  |  Episodes: 48  |  Seasons: 2
Adaptation of the Stories JRPG spinoff. Follows Lute, a young Rider who hatches a Red Rathalos named Rathalos. Bright, adventure-focused tone — closer to a children's action-adventure than the grim survival of the main games. Good introduction to the franchise's lore and monster roster for younger viewers. Streamed on Crunchyroll. Watch alongside or after playing Stories the game; the anime spoils the game's story beats.

📺
Monster Hunter has no anime adaptation of the mainline games. The 2020 live-action film and the 2021 Netflix CGI film cover that space. No mainline anime is currently announced as of 2025.
🎬 Movies
Monster Hunter (2020) — Live Action

Director: Paul W.S. Anderson  |  Cast: Milla Jovovich, Tony Jaa, Ron Perlman
U.S. Army Rangers are transported into the Monster Hunter world through a dimensional portal and must fight to survive. Features Rathalos, Diablos, Nerscylla, and Black Diablos. Anderson brings the same approach he used for Resident Evil — loose adaptation, maximized spectacle. Mixed reviews. Not required viewing but an accessible live-action gateway. Available on digital rental and physical media.

Monster Hunter: Legends of the Guild (2021) — Netflix CGI

Studio: Pure Imagination Studios  |  Streaming: Netflix
CGI animated film following Aiden, a young hunter protecting his village when a massive Elder Dragon migration threatens the region. Acts as a loose prequel to Monster Hunter: World's setting, though not strictly tied to World's story. Shorter (~60 min), family-friendly tone. The better of the two MH films for franchise newcomers. Available on Netflix.

🛒 Merch & Physical Media
📅 2004–2017 — Japanese Collector's Market

Before World, Monster Hunter was primarily a Japanese phenomenon. Capcom produced Japan-exclusive collector's editions, Amiibo-style Monster Hunter Strap figures (Capcom's own capsule toy line), and collaboration merchandise with brands like G-Shock and Uniqlo. The Monster Hunter x Uniqlo line (UT Collection) produced T-shirts for Freedom Unite, 4 Ultimate, and Generations eras — sold only in Japan, now collector's items.

📅 2018–Present — Western Merchandise Explosion

Monster Hunter: World's global success opened the floodgates. Capcom partnered with NECA for high-quality action figures (Rathalos, Zinogre, Rajang lines), Good Smile Company produced Nendoroid and figma figures of hunters and flagship monsters, and Dark Horse published an art book series. The Monster Hunter: World Official Art Works hardcover book is the highest-quality franchise merch available in the West. Funko Pop lines for World and Rise exist but are lower-tier collector value.

🎮 Physical Game Collector's Editions

Notable physical editions include: Monster Hunter: World Collector's Edition (PS4/Xbox — included a Palico model kit and steelbook), Monster Hunter Rise Collector's Edition (Switch — Kamura Nendoroid figure), and the Monster Hunter 20th Anniversary Box (Japan exclusive — multiple game compilation). The Rise CE Nendoroid figure is the most sought-after collector's item in the Western market.

📖 Franchise History
📅 2004–2008 — The PS2 & PSP Foundations
Born on PS2, conquered Japan on PSP
Monster Hunter launched on PS2 in 2004 to modest sales — director Kaname Fujioka and producer Ryozo Tsujimoto had built something unique but hard to market. The game's PSP port and sequels, however, found a perfect home in Japan's commuter culture: bite-sized hunts played with friends during train rides. By 2008, Monster Hunter Freedom Unite on PSP had sold over 4 million copies in Japan alone. The series was a genuine cultural phenomenon in Japan while remaining almost unknown in the West, where sales were a fraction of Japanese numbers.
📅 2009–2013 — The Tri Pivot & 3DS Ascent
Wii reboot and the 3DS takeover
Monster Hunter Tri (2009) was a calculated pivot — Capcom rebuilt the franchise on Wii with a cleaner visual direction, underwater combat, and simplified onboarding. It performed well in the West compared to previous entries. Monster Hunter 3 Ultimate (2011/2012) brought the Tri content to 3DS and WiiU with cross-platform support. Meanwhile, Capcom deepened the 3DS relationship: Monster Hunter 4 (2013, Japan) introduced climbing and mounting mechanics, overhauled the combat loop significantly, and set up the series for its most prolific portable era. The 3DS line built an enormous dedicated fanbase in the West.
📅 2014–2017 — The Portable Pinnacle
4 Ultimate and Generations define the classic era
Monster Hunter 4 Ultimate (2014/2015 West) is widely considered the best game in the classic portable style. It sold over 4 million copies worldwide and introduced online multiplayer on 3DS via Nintendo Wi-Fi. Monster Hunter Generations (2015/2016 West) was the series' 10th anniversary celebration: every Hunting Style and Art system, the return of classic locations, and the introduction of the Fated Four flagship monsters. Generations Ultimate (2017, Switch release 2018) expanded that to a massive G-Rank endgame. This era represented the ceiling of the classic formula — and Capcom knew it needed to reinvent for global audiences.
📅 2018 — The World Breakthrough
Monster Hunter: World changes everything
Monster Hunter: World (January 2018) is one of the defining game launches of the 2010s. Capcom rebuilt the franchise from scratch for HD consoles and PC: seamless open zones replaced load-screen-gated areas, monsters interacted with each other and the environment dynamically, and drop-in co-op worked across platforms. The game sold 5 million copies in its first three days — previously unthinkable for the series outside Japan. It ultimately sold 21+ million copies and became the best-selling game in Capcom's history. The Iceborne expansion (2019) added equivalent volume of new content and pushed total World/Iceborne sales past 25 million combined. The franchise's mainstream identity permanently shifted from "Japanese portable cult hit" to "global action-RPG institution."
📅 2021–2022 — Rise and the Switch Generation
Wirebug, Palamutes, and the fastest entry in the series
Monster Hunter Rise (March 2021) launched as a Switch title designed around the hybrid console's portability. The Wirebug traversal system — a grappling hook with mid-air combat applications — was the fastest, most vertical MH had ever felt. Rise sold 10 million copies in its first year. Sunbreak (2022), the Master Rank expansion, was praised as one of the best expansions in the series. Rise also marked the debut of the Palamute rideable dog companion, replacing the Poogie as the franchise's secondary mascot.
📅 2025 — Present
Monster Hunter Wilds — the current flagship
Monster Hunter Wilds launched in February 2025 to enormous commercial performance — highest day-one concurrent players on Steam for a Capcom title, with the highest pre-order figures in company history. Wilds introduces dynamic weather ecosystems that materially alter hunting conditions, a new Seikret mount, and expanded narrative ambitions. The Forbidden Lands setting is the most visually complex environment in the series. As of 2025, Wilds is the active service game for the franchise. Monster Hunter Now (Niantic, 2023) continues as the active mobile title. The series has sold over 100 million units total across all entries.

🗺️ Canon Map
What's canon? What's connected?

Monster Hunter has no strict connected universe or timeline. Each mainline game takes place in a different region with different characters. The mainline games (MH1 → Wilds) share ecological lore and monster biology but have no shared narrative continuity. The Stories series is a separate canon universe where monsters are partners rather than prey — the anime is Stories-universe, not mainline. The 2020 film is standalone and non-canon. Monster Hunter Online introduced several monsters (Espinas, Goss Harag) that were later canonized into mainline entries — those monsters are canon even if the game's story isn't. Monster Hunter Now is non-canon. The series' lore focus is ecological and encyclopedic (Hunter's Notes, environmental storytelling) rather than narrative.
