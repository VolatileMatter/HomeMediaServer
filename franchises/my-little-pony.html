My Little Pony

// G1–G5 · TV · Movies · GBA · DS · Mobile · Jellyfin //

Hasbro's toy-turned-media empire, running from 1982 to the present across five toy generations and a remarkable diversity of animated media. G1 set the classic tone (ponies vs dark fantasy villains); G4 Friendship Is Magic (2010–2019) redefined it for a generation, attracting an unexpected adult fandom (bronies). G5 launched in 2021 and was discontinued in 2024. Use the Library tab to sort by release order, generation, or platform. Other tabs cover TV/Anime, Movies, Merch history, Tunarr filler, and full franchise history.

🛒 MLP Merchandise History
🌸 G1 Merchandise (1982–1995)

The original toy line — six Earth Ponies in 1982, expanding rapidly to Unicorns, Pegasi, Flutter Ponies, Sea Ponies, and Baby Ponies. Over 150 named G1 ponies produced. Huge collector market today; original condition G1s fetch significant sums. Notable playsets include Dream Castle (1983), Paradise Estate (1986, introduced in the movie), and the Lullaby Nursery. G1 merch was discontinued in the US in 1992, internationally in 1995. The 1985 G1 comic series ran for 220+ issues — one of the most successful licensed comics of its era.

💎 G2 Merchandise (1997–1999)

Redesigned slimmer proportions with jewel eyes and turning heads. Five initial ponies: Berry Bright, Clever Clover, Morning Glory, Petal Blossom, Sky Skimmer. G2 was US-limited and commercially unsuccessful; sold more widely in Europe. Discontinued 1999. Almost no media to speak of — a small 5-issue European comic and the Friendship Gardens PC game are the sum total of G2 non-toy output. Extremely rare in the US collector market today.

🌺 G3 Merchandise (2003–2009)

Return to approachable, rounder pony designs. Targeted younger audience than G1. ~100 million G3 ponies sold globally by 2010. Major playsets include the Celebration Castle and Ponyville town sets. G3 included a "G3.5" mini-refresh in 2009 with chibi-style proportions (bridging toward G4). The G3 design is closest to what most adults picture when they think "classic My Little Pony."

🌟 G4 Merchandise (2010–2020)

The franchise's commercial apex. Over $1 billion in annual retail sales in 2014–2015. G4 introduced the "Mane Six" as a stable character lineup, enabling collectible series targeting adult bronies alongside the core child demographic. Notable sub-lines: blind bags, Funko vinyl figures, NECA plush, Hot Topic exclusives, San Diego Comic-Con convention exclusives, the collector-aimed Guardians of Harmony figures, and the IDW comic series (2012–2021, 220+ issues). The 2017 movie tie-in merch was a commercial success in its own right.

🌊 G5 Merchandise (2021–2024) [Discontinued]

Spin Master acquired the MLP toy license from Hasbro for G5, replacing Hasbro's own manufacturing. G5 failed to replicate G4's commercial success — lower product sales, mixed reviews, and inability to capture the brony fanbase (which remained G4-focused). Officially discontinued in 2024. Hasbro announced a "Total Brand Reinvention" in May 2025, aiming for a 2026–2027 relaunch with "retro-vibes" styling. A live-action Amazon MGM film was announced July 2025.

📖 My Little Pony — Franchise History
1981–1982 — Origin: My Pretty Pony

Illustrator Bonnie Zacherle and sculptor Charles Muenchinger created the original "My Pretty Pony" figurine for Hasbro in 1981 — a 10-inch hard plastic toy that could wiggle ears, swish its tail, and wink. It was marketed to both boys and girls. The commercial relaunch in 1982 downsized the design, added vivid colors and flank symbols, and launched the brand that would define a generation of girls' toys. The initial six-pony lineup (Blossom, Butterscotch, Cotton Candy, Minty, Snuzzle, Bluebelle) sold out immediately.

1984–1992 — G1: Animation and Expansion

Hasbro partnered with Sunbow Productions and Marvel Productions to produce animated content, animated by Toei Animation. The 1984 special "Rescue at Midnight Castle" introduced a surprisingly dark high-fantasy tone — the villain Tirac was genuinely menacing. By 1986, the franchise was large enough to support a theatrical film (with Danny DeVito, Madeline Kahn) and a full syndicated anthology series "My Little Pony 'n Friends." The 1985 Marvel/Star Comics series ran 220+ issues. The G1 animated universe featured three human children (Megan, Danny, and Molly) who frequently crossed the "Rainbow" to Ponyland — a distinctive element later generations dropped. The toy line itself grew to encompass over 150 individually named ponies across multiple sub-types before declining in the early 1990s, ending US production in 1992 and international production in 1995.

1997–1999 — G2: The Quiet Failure

Hasbro relaunched MLP in 1997 with a redesigned European-influenced look — smaller, slimmer proportions, jewel eyes. The G2 toyline performed poorly in the US and was discontinued by 1999, though European sales continued somewhat longer. There was almost no media output: a short European comic run and the 1998 PC game "Friendship Gardens" (developed by Artech Studios) represent the entirety of G2 non-toy content. G2 remains the least-documented and least-collected generation.

2003–2009 — G3: Ponyville and the DTV Era

A more commercially successful relaunch with toy designs resembling the original G1 aesthetic. Ponyville was established as the main setting. SD Entertainment produced a series of direct-to-video animated films — lighter in tone than G1, targeting a younger audience. Approximately 100 million G3 toys were sold globally by 2010. The generation received a design refresh mid-run (sometimes called "G3.5" or "Core 7"), narrowing the main cast to seven core ponies. Lauren Faust, who would later create FiM, was shown the G3 "Princess Promenade" special when she approached Hasbro with her "Galaxy Girls" pitch — a key coincidence that led to G4.

2010–2019 — G4: Friendship Is Magic and the Brony Phenomenon

Animator Lauren Faust (The Powerpuff Girls, Foster's Home for Imaginary Friends) was hired to develop the G4 relaunch. Her pitch bible established deeper characterization, fantasy worldbuilding, and a fully female main cast with genuine personality differentiation. The Hub network premiered "Friendship Is Magic" on October 10, 2010. Within months, the show had attracted an unexpected adult male audience — "bronies" — who organized online through forums, fan conventions (BronyCon, 2011–2019), fan music, animation, and a thriving creative community. The brand grossed over $1 billion annually at retail in 2014–2015. G4 ran for 9 seasons (221 episodes), spawned the Equestria Girls spin-off franchise, a 2017 theatrical film, an IDW comic series, and multiple games. The series finale aired October 12, 2019.

2020–2024 — G5: A New Generation, and a New Discontinuation

G5 launched with the Netflix-exclusive 3D CGI film "A New Generation" in September 2021, produced by Entertainment One and animated by Boulder Media. Set generations after G4 in a world where the three pony tribes had forgotten each other, the film was warmly received. The follow-up Netflix series "Make Your Mark" and short-form "Tell Your Tale" series continued the G5 story through 2023–2024. However, G5 failed to capture the cultural moment G4 had — the brony fandom remained G4-focused, toy sales underperformed, and the line was officially discontinued in 2024. A planned reboot ("Camp Cutie Mark," bridging G4 and G5 characters) was leaked and subsequently cancelled by Hasbro in September 2024. In May 2025, Hasbro announced a "Total Brand Reinvention" for 2026–2027 and a live-action film via Amazon MGM Studios.
