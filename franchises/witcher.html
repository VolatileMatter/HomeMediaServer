The Witcher

// PC · PS4/5 · Switch · iOS/Android · Netflix · Books · Comics //

Andrzej Sapkowski's dark fantasy saga about Geralt of Rivia — a mutant monster hunter navigating a world where humans are often more monstrous than the beasts. Born in Polish literary magazines in 1986, the franchise became a global phenomenon through CD Projekt Red's RPG trilogy, with The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt (2015) widely considered one of the greatest games ever made. 📖 Franchise History
The Continent's Story — How a Polish short story became a global franchise

The Witcher's journey from a 1986 Fantastyka magazine competition to one of the world's most recognized fantasy franchises is genuinely unlikely. Andrzej Sapkowski entered a short story on a whim, urged by his son — and won third prize. The result was "The Witcher" (Wiedźmin), a subversive fairy-tale riff about a mutant monster-hunter that rejected the conventions of heroic fantasy. Poland fell in love. The rest of the world took another two decades to catch up.

📅 Franchise Timeline
📅 1986–1999 — The Book Era
Sapkowski Builds the Continent
Sapkowski published two short story collections (The Last Wish, 1993; Sword of Destiny, 1992) and five novels (Blood of Elves through Lady of the Lake, 1994–1999). The saga was a phenomenon in Poland and across Central and Eastern Europe, but remained largely unknown in the West due to limited translations. Sapkowski concluded the saga in 1999 with Lady of the Lake and largely stepped away — he would not return with new Witcher material until 2013.
📅 2001–2002 — The Failed Adaptation
The Hexer: A Cautionary Tale
Polish TV produced a theatrical film and 13-episode series based on the short stories. Both were critically derided — the film was an incoherent compilation of unreleased TV footage, and the series overcorrected in ways that deeply departed from the source material. Sapkowski famously dismissed both in one obscene word. The experience soured him on adaptations for years and made him initially reluctant to deal with CD Projekt Red when they approached in 2003.
📅 2007–2012 — CD Projekt Red Changes Everything
The Games Arrive
CD Projekt Red (a Polish game developer) licensed the rights and released The Witcher in 2007 — their first game, and it received strong reviews in Europe and North America. Sapkowski famously signed away the rights for a flat fee, claiming not to believe in games, a decision he later called "stupid." The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings (2011) expanded the console audience with an Xbox 360 version and established CDPR as a world-class RPG developer. These games introduced Geralt to an international audience that had never read the books.
📅 2015–2016 — The Wild Hunt Moment
One of the Greatest Games Ever Made
The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt (2015) was a cultural moment. Over 800 game of the year awards. A world that felt genuinely alive. Moral choices with real consequences. The Hearts of Stone and Blood and Wine DLCs in 2015–2016 extended what many considered the best game of its generation. TW3 sold over 50 million copies and singlehandedly elevated the franchise from a European cult property to a global phenomenon. The series' books sold out worldwide after the game launched.
📅 2018–2021 — The Card Games and Netflix Expansion
GWENT, Thronebreaker, and Henry Cavill
GWENT (the beloved minigame from TW3) became a standalone card game in 2018, and Thronebreaker (a narrative RPG using GWENT mechanics) demonstrated CDPR's ability to tell compelling non-Geralt stories. Netflix launched The Witcher series in 2019 with Henry Cavill as Geralt — it became one of Netflix's biggest originals, reportedly 76 million households in its first four weeks. The animated film Nightmare of the Wolf (2021) followed. The franchise was at its commercial peak.
📅 2021–2023 — Turbulence and Loss
Mobile Failures and the Cavill Departure
The Witcher: Monster Slayer (2021) was a well-built AR mobile game that simply failed to retain players — shut down in June 2023. Henry Cavill's shock departure from the Netflix series (announced October 2022) dominated entertainment news and divided the fanbase. CDPR simultaneously announced three new Witcher games — TW4 (Polaris), a Witcher remake (Canis Majoris), and a spinoff (Sirius) — signaling a long-term future even as short-term turbulence hit.
📅 2024–Present — The Ciri Era Begins
Witcher 4 Reveal and the New Trilogy
The Witcher 4 was officially revealed at The Game Awards in December 2024 with a stunning cinematic trailer confirming Ciri as the new protagonist. CDPR's March 2025 earnings report confirmed the game won't ship before 2027. Sapkowski published Crossroads of Ravens in 2024 — his first new Witcher novel in 11 years. Netflix's Season 4 arrived with Liam Hemsworth; Season 5 (the final season) is in production. The franchise is actively building toward a new era even as the Geralt chapter draws to a close on both screens and in games.

🗺️ Canon Map
Three separate canons — they share a world but not a timeline

Sapkowski's Books: The original and most authoritative canon. The games are explicitly non-canonical sequels according to Sapkowski himself — they share characters and lore but diverge from the book ending. The Netflix series is "inspired by" the books, not a direct adaptation, and has its own continuity with different timeline dates.

CD Projekt Red Games: Their own canon. The Witcher 1–3 are set roughly 5 years after Lady of the Lake and treat the books as backstory. Thronebreaker is canon to the game universe. The Witcher 4 (Ciri protagonist) continues this game canon.

Netflix Universe: Adapts book events with different dates, character changes, and story beats. Nightmare of the Wolf, Blood Origin, Sirens of the Deep, and The Rats are all Netflix canon. The Polish Hexer (2001–2002) is not canonical to either.
