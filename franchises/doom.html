DOOM

// PC · Consoles · Mobile · Books · Movies · Comics · Jellyfin //

The grandfather of the first-person shooter. id Software's DOOM (1993) defined a genre, spawned a decade of clones, and built the modern PC gaming market. The franchise spans five mainline entries, dozens of ports, two feature films (one fan-made, one theatrical), novelizations, comics, board games, and a vibrant modding community that keeps the original alive. 📖 A History of DOOM
1991–1993 — Birth of id Software

John Carmack, John Romero, Adrian Carmack, and Tom Hall left Softdisk to form id Software. After Wolfenstein 3D, they developed a new engine for a darker, faster game. DOOM was distributed via shareware (episode 1 free). Within two years, it was installed on more PCs than Windows 95. "Doom" defined the FPS genre.

2004 — DOOM 3: Horror Reboot

id Tech 4 powered a slower, survival-horror reimagining. Polarized fans but commercially successful. Introduced the BFG Edition and Resurrection of Evil.

2016 — The Renaissance

After a decade of dormancy, id Software (now under Bethesda) released DOOM (2016) — a return to fast-paced combat, glory kills, and Mick Gordon's iconic soundtrack. It won numerous Game of the Year awards.

2020–2021 — DOOM Eternal & The Ancient Gods

Eternal expanded mechanics with dashes, flame belch, and resource management. The Ancient Gods expansions concluded the Slayer saga. The franchise is currently resting after a massive success.
