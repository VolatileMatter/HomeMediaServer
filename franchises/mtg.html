Magic: The Gathering

// CCG · Digital · Books · Comics · Web Series · Arena · Netflix (Canceled) //

The world's first trading card game, created by Richard Garfield and published by Wizards of the Coast in 1993. MTG spans 30+ years of lore across the Multiverse — Dominaria, Ravnica, Innistrad, and countless planes. Beyond cards, the franchise includes novels, comics, web series, audio dramas, video games (from Shandalar to Arena), and a canceled Netflix series. 📖 History of Magic: The Gathering
1993 — Invention & Alpha

Richard Garfield designs "Magic" as a game to play between roleplaying sessions. Wizards of the Coast releases Alpha, sells out in weeks. Creates the TCG genre.

1994–2000 — Expansion Era & The Weatherlight Saga

Arabian Nights, Antiquities, the Weatherlight Saga story arc. Pro Tour launched 1996.

2010–2020 — Digital Transformation & Arena

MTG Arena beta 2018, becomes dominant platform. Netfix series announced (later canceled).

2020–Present — Universes Beyond & 30th Anniversary

Crossover sets with Lord of the Rings, Warhammer, Doctor Who. 30th Anniversary celebrated 2023. Future: continued digital dominance, no announced screen adaptations after Netflix collapse.
